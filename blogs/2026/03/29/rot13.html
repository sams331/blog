Caesar cipher is one of the simplest and one of the most famous encryption techniques. Caesar cipher works by replacing plain text with a corresponding letter in the alphabet that has been shifted to left or right several times. For example, caesar cipher with a shift of 2 to the right means A will be X, B will be Y, C will be Z, D will be A, and so on. This encryption method is named after Julius Caesar, who used it in his private correspondence.

ROT13 is one of Caesar cipher's technique. ROT13 means rotate 13, which means that a letter is replaced to the 13th letter after it in the alphabet. That means, A will be N, B will be O, C will be P, and so on.
